Workload (after class):

  1. Together with your course partner, decide on a research topic and question that aligns with the main course topic: Natural Disasters in Historical Newspapers. Use the German Digital Newspaper Portal (Deutsches Zeitungsportal) to explore available historical newspapers. Potential topics and research questions could include:

    • Natural Disasters and Migration:
      How did newspapers report on displacement and population movements following natural disasters?
    • The Heat Crisis of 1911:
      How did agricultural crisis reporting vary across different regions and/or newspapers?
    • Vesuvius Eruption of 1906:
      - How did newspapers utilize telegraph networks for real-time updates?
      - How did papers cover the response of local authorities?
    • Messina Earthquake 1908:
      - How did newspapers report on the organization and effectiveness of international aid efforts after the Messina earthquake?
    • Natural Disasters in War Time:
      - How did wartime conditions influence disaster reporting and response?
    • The 1910 European floods:
      - How did newspapers cover the vulnerability of modern urban infrastructure?
